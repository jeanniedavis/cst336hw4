The 8-bit video game era is considered the 3rd generation of video games. It was an era where video games started to become more advanced in colors, sound, and processing power. In 1983, Nintendo released the Famicom in Japan, this was 2 years later released in North America as the Nintendo Enterainment System(NES) alongside Super Mario Bros, which is known as one of the most famous video game series of all time. The NES was the most successful console of the 8-bit era, but there are a few other companies who released consoles and video games. Some of the rivals of the NES were the following:

Company/Console Game
Sega Master System (Mark III) Aladdin (1994)
Atari 7800 Dig Dug (1986)

In 1989 Nintendo released the Game Boy which was an 8-bit handheld console. The title of the games that came out during the North America launch were the following:

  • Alleyway
  • Baseball
  • Super Mario Land
  • Tennis
  • Tetris

Some of the most famous games that were released for the Game Boy are the following:

  • Donkey Kong (1994)
  • Pokemon (1998)
  • Warrior Land (1994)
  • Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening (1993)
  • The Final Fantasy Legend (1990)
  • Kirby's Dream Land (1992)
